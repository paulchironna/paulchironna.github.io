Rigol DS1054Z Oscilloscope Repair

This DS1054Z Oscilloscope was displaying a fuzzy, distorted, and poorly attenuated waveform on all 4 channels using known-good probes.

Initial waveform issue observed on CH1 using clean 1KHz square wave signal

Initial Findings

Upon disassembling the oscilloscope, I immediately noticed pinched wires in the area where the main harness was fed through the power supply EMI shield. Luckily, the insulation was still intact and the damaged power wires hadn't shorted to ground. Couldn't be that easy, right?

Pinched wires
Repaired and heat shrunk wires

Analog Board Inspection

Unfortunately, it wasn't that easy. While this was still a necessary repair, the same waveform pictured above appeared when I retested. So my next step was to isolate the analog board, inject my 1KHz test signal, and see if I could trace it from the center pin on the BNC jack through the attenuator circuit. However, I didn't get very far...

Close-up of CH1 input path
Probing BNC center pin

Burned-out Resistors

After injecting my signal and measuring right off the center pin, I captured the ugly waveform pictured below. The issue had to be right there! The solder joints on the jack seemed okay, but something seemed off about the resistor that lays in series between the input and attenuator relay. So I broke out my microscope to find that this resistor had clearly failed. I verified that the resistor was open with a multimeter and repeated the process on all 4 channels with the same result. Bingo!

Waveform captured right after the BNC input pin
Blown resistor on CH1 input path, likely due to overvoltage/current condition

Soldering

With the failed resistors identified, I carefully desoldered them, cleaned the area thoroughly, and replaced them with matched-value parts. I then injected my test signal, grounded the board, and verified that my square wave was now making its way to the relay.

New SMD resistor soldered onto CH2 input path
Test setup to verify repair

Success!

With the new resistors installed and connections verified, I powered the scope back on and ran test signals into all four channels. Each channel now properly displays a clean 1KHz square wave. Problem solved!

All 4 channels properly displaying the waveform
